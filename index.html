Viscoelastic Mayonnaise — Simulation Gallery

These clips visualise the steady-state shear and torsion fields of the fractional Kelvin–Voigt (FKV) model, animated as u = Re{U₀ e^{iωt}} over one forcing period. The planar runs solve the 2D antiplane shear problem on a 50 × 10 mm section with a 5 mm soft inclusion; the cylindrical runs solve the axisymmetric torsion of a 50 mm × 10 mm rheometer gap. Material parameters are the fitted MayoB matrix (G = 1000 Pa, η_α = 1500 Pa·sᵅ, α = 0.13) and the softer fitted MayoA1 inclusion (η_α = 213 Pa·sᵅ, α = 0.10) — the same values used throughout the report.

One choice here is made for visibility: the damped-torsion clips run at ω = 300 rad/s, above the measured rheometer window (0.06–63 rad/s) and close to the torsional resonance (~314 rad/s), so that the through-thickness phase lag and the near-resonant amplitude growth are actually visible. At in-window frequencies the material twists quasi-statically with no perceptible lag; the ω = 10 rad/s baseline clip shows that realistic regime.
